Understanding ADHD

If your child has recently been diagnosed with ADHD, or you're still waiting for an assessment, you're probably looking for clear, honest answers. What does this diagnosis actually mean? Will it affect how they learn and grow? What can you do to help? These are the questions we hear most, and our specialist videos are designed to answer them properly.

ADHD, or Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder, affects the way the brain manages attention, impulse control, and activity levels. It looks different in every child. Some children are visibly restless and impulsive; others are quietly distracted and hard to reach. Many are bright and creative, with strengths that a traditional classroom doesn't always recognise.

The videos on this page are produced with New Zealand specialists who assess, diagnose, and support children with ADHD every day. They cover everything from first questions at diagnosis through to medication, school strategies, and the experiences of girls and young people who often present differently.

You don't need to watch everything at once. Use the sections below to find what's most relevant to where you are right now, whether you're just starting to look into ADHD, preparing for a school meeting, or supporting a teenager who has questions of their own.
