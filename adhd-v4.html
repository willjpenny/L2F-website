Understanding ADHD

Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) is one of the most common neurodevelopmental conditions affecting children and young people in New Zealand. It affects the way the brain develops and works, making it harder to focus, control impulses, and manage activity levels. ADHD is not a result of poor parenting or lack of effort — it is a recognised medical condition with effective, evidence-based supports.

ADHD looks different in every child. Some children are hyperactive and impulsive; others are primarily inattentive and may go undiagnosed for years, particularly girls. Many children experience a mix of both. With the right understanding and support at home and school, children with ADHD can thrive.

The videos and resources on this page have been created by specialist clinicians and families with lived experience of ADHD. Whether you are newly diagnosed, supporting a child at school, or looking for guidance on medication, you will find expert advice here — in plain language, at no cost.
