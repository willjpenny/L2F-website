Dark teal field. Colourful speech and thought bubbles — yellow, light teal, off-white — appear and fade independently, each carrying a real family question. The panel becomes a living version of the LTF brand poster.

The visual material runs through the whole panel without interruption. No dividers, no zones. Every bubble is the same design language as the left panel — the hero feels like one continuous surface.

Strength: brand-native, always coherent, zero dependency on photo quality
Gap: doesn't explicitly show faces — families and specialists are implied, not visible

One portrait fills the panel at a time. The cycle alternates: a family member appears with their question in a speech bubble — then a specialist appears with their area of expertise. One clear story per moment.

When real curated stills replace the silhouettes, this becomes the highest-impact option. The full-bleed face creates an immediate human connection. The left panel's speech bubbles are mirrored here — the format rhymes.

Strength: premium feel, emotionally direct, no competing elements
Gap: one message at a time — visitor needs to wait through 2–3 frames for the full picture
Best if: you can supply 4–6 strong portrait stills (close crop, clear expression)
